How the billing works (tap to expand)

Two meters, added together. Availability = renting the committed plane (daily rate; covers ALL loading, preflight, waiting, unloading). Flight = using it (block-to-block clock minutes). Both run at the same time; flying does not pause the window. No tax is ever added.

The 15 minutes is the meter's tick size, not a task box. DA $6,216 / 14-hr day = $444.00/hr = exactly $111.00 per 15-min block (that is the divisible-by-56 rule working). IBS keys it as a full AV day $6,216 minus NA hours @ $444; same math, same net. A 12-minute load can't be "lost": the meter was already running when it happened. Temporary release per the TO: no availability paid, we fly our own work, back within 24 hrs of the COR's call.

Weather cancel: bill the window (or the ordered day), zero flight. Engine started then cancelled, or ordered ramp reposition: flat 0.1 hr, entered as a 6-minute leg with the reason. That is the only 6-minute rule anywhere; flight time itself is exact, never rounded, never Hobbs.

Never: bill Hobbs; bill loading or unloading as line items; add FET; bill availability over time the plane flew AKS commercial work; bill anything without an order.

  5. Drag the file into the box above.

3 · Availability window

FS clock on Flight legs FS clock off
Clock ON is not block-out: it starts earlier, when we begin working their order (preflight and loading are paid time). Clock OFF is when the job is done and the plane is ours again.
Availability window parameters (verbal agreement with the COR; why each allowance is what it is)

Governing principle: the same hour of work is never billed to two customers. The full daily pre-flight or post-flight belongs to whichever mission it is actually for. If the FS trip is the plane's first or last flight of the day, that work is theirs; mid-day, they owe only what their mission adds.

Window OPENS before first block-outMinCovers

More flying after, standard unload15Passengers in, bags and cargo off
More flying after, heavy unload (500 lbs or more) or seat reinstall30Above plus cargo handling and reconfiguration

Rules: any started 15-minute block bills as a full block (30 min bills 30; 31 or 32 bills 45; 46 bills 60). Actual logged times govern when the mission runs longer. Ground time at remote stops is already inside the window. A window stated on the order or by the COR overrides these defaults. Outside ordered windows the aircraft returns to our schedule and no availability is billed.

Worked examples: mid-day heavy mission (aircraft flew commercial that morning, 500+ lbs with seat work, nothing after): 45 pre + 45 post; blocks 12:24 to 14:34 give a window of 11:39 to 15:19 = 15 blocks = $1,665.00. First flight of the day with commercial flying after (heavy retrieval): 60 pre + 30 post; blocks 10:26 to 11:42 give 09:26 to 12:12 = 12 blocks = $1,332.00.
